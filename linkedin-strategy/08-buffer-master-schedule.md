**Type:** Technical Insight

```
Hot take: serverless isn't just a cost optimization strategy. It's an accessibility strategy.

When you're building for users in regions with unreliable infrastructure, you need:
- Zero cold-start dependencies
- Pay-per-use (because traffic is unpredictable)
- Global edge distribution
- No servers to maintain on the ground

AWS Lambda + API Gateway + DynamoDB + Vercel isn't just a trendy stack. For some use cases, it's the only architecture that makes sense.

What's your go-to stack for building for emerging markets?

#Serverless #AWS #CloudArchitecture #TechForGood #Engineering

### POST 08 | Sat Mar 8 | 10:00 AM | MEYNG PAGE

**Image:** `png/07-post-tech-stack.png`
**Type:** Behind the Tech

```
Building AI for underserved communities comes with unique technical challenges. Here's what powers SangoAI under the hood:

The stack:
Frontend: React + TypeScript + Vite (deployed on Vercel)
Backend: AWS Lambda (Python) — fully serverless
AI: Claude 3.5 Sonnet via AWS Bedrock
Auth: AWS Cognito with Google OAuth
Database: DynamoDB for sessions and usage tracking
Payments: Stripe + MTN Mobile Money (for African users)

The hardest part? Training AI to understand a language with limited digital corpus. Sango has almost no presence in existing AI training data.

And we designed everything as a PWA — because many users access the web on low-bandwidth connections with basic devices.

Technology choices matter. We chose every tool with our users' constraints in mind.

#SangoAI #Serverless #AWS #React #AIEngineering #MEYNG

### POST 17 | Mon Mar 24 | 9:00 AM | PERSONAL PROFILE

**Image:** `png/22-post-pm-to-builder.png`
**Type:** Career Reflection

```
From project manager to AI builder: a few things nobody tells you.

After 14+ years managing enterprise IT programs, I started building AI products on the side. Here's what surprised me:

1. Enterprise skills transfer. Stakeholder management, scoping, prioritization — they matter just as much in a startup of one.

2. The hardest part isn't the code. It's deciding what NOT to build. Scope creep hits harder when you're the PM and the developer.

3. Shipping > perfection. My first deploy had bugs. My users didn't care. They cared that the tool existed at all.

4. Side projects teach you more in 6 months than 3 years of enterprise work. Not because enterprise is bad — but because you own every decision.

If you're thinking about building something alongside your career — just start.

#CareerGrowth #SideProject #AI #Builder #LessonsLearned
```
